4 # Timers (milli seconds and micro seconds) {#kinetis_timer}
5 For stable communication with A71CH, appropriate timings have to be respected by the host.
6 - `timer_kinetis.c `
7 - `timer_kinetis_freertos.c ` and
8 - `timer_kinetis_bm.c `
9 
10 implements such timer handling. Rather than explicitly reserving/using timers from the controller, to simplify the porting effort the implementation uses SysTick timers for millisecond delays and internal loops for microsecond delays. During porting, sanity of such delays have to be ascertained.
11 
12 ## ARM SysTick – baremetal design{#ARM_systick}
13 For integration where FreeRTOS is not involved and bare metal design is used, the integration uses ARM SysTick timer to be configured for 1ms and the SysTick call back is used by the integration to keep track of 1milli seconds delay.
14 (Reference `timer_kinetis_bm.c`)
15 
16 ## ARM SysTick – with FreeRTOS design {#ARM_systick_freertos}
17 For integration where FreeRTOS is involved, SysTick itself could be used by FreeRTOS.
18 It is assumed that SysTick is configured for 1ms delay so that sm_sleep() api could easily
19 fall back on vTaskDelay() for expected milli seconds delay.
20 For testing and host library examples, the delay in milliseconds is also tracked. In such cases in 'FreeRTOSConfig.h`
21 (FreeRTOS configuration file) must enable configUSE_TICK_HOOK so that vApplicationTickHook could be used to keep timing information .
22 (Reference `timer_kinetis_freertos.c`)
